Introduction

Push-pull-legs is quite flexible and can accomdate fluctuations in your schedule.

  • Busy week? You only need to go to the gym three times a week to hit every muscle in the body!
  • Chill week? You can go to the gym six times a week and hit every muscle twice a week!
  • Focused on chest gain? Throw in one extra push day per week!

The problem with the bro split is that it relies on hitting the gym at least five times a week: chest, back, shoulders, arms, legs. But if you were to miss a couple of workouts, do you resume from where you left off the next week or simply just reset so you can do chest on international chest day? Additionally, it has been shown that the frequency in which you hit a muscle will best stimulate growth. Hence the growing popularity now with push-pull-legs as well as full body. I think bro splits used to be popular because you could achieve a satisfying pump for each day. But science has also shown that once we exceed a certain number of sets of a muscle, we start to have junk volume. See the video below for more information on junk volume.

TLDW? Try not to exceed 10 sets per muscle group in a workout.
Overall, push-pull-leg is great for both beginners and intermediate lifters! Have fun!
